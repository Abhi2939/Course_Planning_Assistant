COURSE_ID: CS 233
TITLE: CS 233 Computer Architecture

DESCRIPTION:
Fundamentals of computer architecture: digital logic design, working up from the logic gate level to understand the function of a simple computer; machine-level programming to understand implementation of high-level languages; performance models of modern computer architectures to enable performance optimization of software; hardware primitives for parallelism and security.

PREREQUISITES:
CS 125 or CS 128; CS 173 or MATH 213; credit or concurrent enrollment in CS 225

COURSE_ID: CS 307
TITLE: CS 307 Modeling and Learning in Data Science

DESCRIPTION:
Introduction to the use of classical approaches in data modeling and machine learning in the context of solving data-centric problems. A broad coverage of fundamental models is presented, including linear models, unsupervised learning, supervised learning, and deep learning. A significant emphasis is placed on the application of the models in Python and the interpretability of the results.
